# Tegmentum

Functionality:
- Contains nuclei for motor control, reward processing, and autonomic regulation

What affects it:
- Cortical and subcortical inputs
- Sensory feedback
- Neurotransmitter modulation

How it affects other parts:
- Influences movement via red nucleus and substantia nigra
- Modulates arousal and motivation through dopamine
- Affects pain perception via periaqueductal gray

Explanation:
- The tegmentum, the ventral midbrain region, houses several important nuclei. The red nucleus coordinates limb movements, particularly reaching and grasping. The substantia nigra produces dopamine for the basal ganglia, essential for smooth movement and reward learning. The periaqueductal gray modulates pain and defensive behaviors. The tegmentum also contains the locus coeruleus (norepinephrine) and raphe nuclei (serotonin), contributing to arousal and mood. This region integrates motor planning with motivational states, enabling goal-directed behavior.

- Implement dopamine signaling for motivation
