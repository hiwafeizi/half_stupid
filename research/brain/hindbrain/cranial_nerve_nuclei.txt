# Cranial Nerve Nuclei

Functionality:
- Control sensory and motor functions of the head, neck, and viscera via cranial nerves

What affects it:
- Sensory inputs from cranial nerves (vision, hearing, taste, etc.)
- Motor commands from cortex and brainstem
- Reflex arcs for protective responses

How it affects other parts:
- Innervate facial muscles, tongue, and pharynx
- Relay sensory information to appropriate brain areas
- Coordinate swallowing, speech, and eye movements

Explanation:
- Cranial nerve nuclei are clusters of neurons in the brainstem that serve as origins or terminations for the 12 cranial nerves. They are organized somatotopically and functionally, with motor nuclei controlling voluntary movements and sensory nuclei relaying information. For example, the trigeminal nucleus processes facial sensation, the facial nucleus controls facial expressions, and the vestibular nucleus maintains balance. These nuclei integrate with higher brain centers for complex functions like speech and swallowing, while also mediating reflexes like the corneal reflex or gag reflex. Their strategic location in the brainstem allows rapid processing of head and neck information.

- Model as specialized motor and sensory processors for head/neck
- Implement reflex arcs for protective responses
- Simulate coordination of facial and visceral functions
